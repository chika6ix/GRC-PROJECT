# Project 4 — Third-Party Vendor Risk Assessment Programme

## Overview
Built a structured TPRM programme at PRMP Digital covering vendor tiering, security questionnaires, contractual review, and ongoing monitoring.

## Vendor Tiering Model

| Tier | Criteria | Assessment | Review |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Access to sensitive data OR critical systems OR significant operational dependency | Full due diligence | Annual |
| Tier 2 | Moderate access or impact | Standard questionnaire + certification review | Biennial |
| Tier 3 | Commodity suppliers, minimal access | Lightweight self-attestation | On renewal |

## Assessment Domains (Tier 1)

1. **Information Security** — ISO 27001/SOC 2 certifications, access controls, encryption
2. **Data Protection** — Data handling, residency, cross-border transfers, breach notification
3. **Operational Resilience** — BCP, RPO/RTO, DR testing
4. **Contractual & Legal** — DPA terms, right-to-audit, liability, data retention
5. **Fourth-Party Risk** — Subprocessor disclosure, fourth-party management

## Key Assessment Questions

- Does the vendor hold ISO 27001 or SOC 2 Type II certification?
- Where is data stored and processed? Are cross-border transfers compliant with GDPR/NDPA?
- What is the vendor's breach notification procedure and timeline?
- Does the contract include a right-to-audit clause?
- Who are the vendor's subprocessors and how are they managed?
- What is the vendor's RTO and RPO for critical services?

## Outcomes
- Vendor register established with all vendors tiered
- 12 previously unrecorded third-party risks identified and treated
- All Tier 1 vendors assessed in first cycle
- Contractual gaps remediated including right-to-audit clauses and updated DPAs
